About Me

I'm a senior undergraduate at the Indian Institute of Technology Madras, majoring in Computer Science and Engineering, with a minor in English Literature. My academic interests lie broadly in Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence, more particularly in the area of Natural Language Processing. I'm especially intrigued by the practical aspects of these fields, and the interpretability of observations and inferences made on large networks and datasets. Consequently, I've also developed a liking for Social Network Analysis, as well as Graph Theory and Algorithms.

I am from the South Indian metropolis of Chennai, and I've spent a huge chunk of my life in this wonderful city, right from my pre-school days up to college, although I was born in the city of Mumbai (or Bombay, as it was known then), in India, and lived my first two years in Muscat, Oman.

I completed the first phase of my schooling – up till the tenth grade – at Bhavan's Rajaji Vidyashram in Kilpauk, following which I moved to Maharishi Vidya Mandir in the adjoining neighbourhood of Chetpet, to completed my final two years. I've spent the subsequent three and a half years as well in this city, where I'm currently pursuing a bachelor's degree (with Honors) in Computer Science and Engineering, at IIT Madras.
